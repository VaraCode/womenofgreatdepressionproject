Migrant Homes

"They have built homes here out of nothing... They have planted trees and flowers. These flimsy shacks represent many a last stand to maintain self-respect."

Dorothea Lange

The 1930s was a time of great upheaval and migration for millions of people in the United States. The market crash that led to the Great Depression, reduced crop prices so signficantly that "many farmers went bankrupt and lost their farms."


Photo:

"Migrant workers' shack. California. 1935."

1935

The New York Public Library Digital Collections


Sadly, the conditions awaiting migrants were less than desirable. In makeshift camps, they created their homes from "corrugated iron or cardboard." There were few or no "decent sanitary facilities."



Photo:

"Migratory field worker's home on the edge of a pea field. The family lived here through the winter. Imperial Valley, California."

1937

The New York Public Library Digital Collections


Dorothea Lange was one of many photographers employed by the Farm Security Administration to capture images of the plight of the rural poor. The goal was for these images to "persuade political leaders to allocate aid."
